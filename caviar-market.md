<p><p>The caviar market includes several distinct types, each with unique characteristics. Malossol caviar is lightly salted, allowing for a delicate flavor. Pressed caviar is produced by compressing eggs, intensifying their taste and making it spreadable. Salted caviar, typically heavily salted, enhances preservation and flavor but may alter the texture. Pasteurized caviar undergoes heat treatment, extending shelf life while sometimes affecting its taste and texture. Collectively, these types cater to different consumer preferences and culinary applications.</p></p>
