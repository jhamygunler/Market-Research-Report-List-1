<p><p>Variable Optical Delay Lines (VODLs) are devices used to manipulate the timing of optical signals in various applications, such as telecommunications and optical testing. The market for VODLs comprises two main types: Manual VODLs, which require manual adjustment to set the delay, and Motorized VODLs, which utilize motors for automated, precise delay adjustments. Manual VODLs are often more cost-effective but less precise, while Motorized VODLs offer higher accuracy and ease of use, catering to different needs in optical system design and testing.</p></p>
