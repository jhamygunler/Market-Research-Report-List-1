<p><p>The Percutaneous Ventricular Assist Devices (PVADs) market is categorized into two main types: Insertion PVADs and Peripheral PVADs. Insertion PVADs are designed to be directly implanted into the heart to support its function during surgery or acute heart failure situations. Peripheral PVADs, on the other hand, are typically used to support systemic circulation and are less invasive, allowing for easier application in patients with heart issues. Both types aim to improve blood flow and patient outcomes in critical care settings.</p></p>
