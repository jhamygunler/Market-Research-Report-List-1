<p><p>The polymers used in medical devices encompass various materials, including PVC, PP, PS, PE, and TPE, each serving distinct roles. PVC (polyvinyl chloride) is valued for its flexibility and durability, while PP (polypropylene) offers chemical resistance and lightweight properties. PS (polystyrene) is commonly utilized for clear packaging and disposable items. PE (polyethylene) is favored for its toughness and moisture resistance. TPE (thermoplastic elastomers) combines rubber-like flexibility with processability, making it ideal for components requiring elasticity. Together, these materials enhance device performance and safety in healthcare applications.</p></p>
