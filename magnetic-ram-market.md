<p><p>Magnetic RAM (MRAM) is a type of non-volatile memory with two key market types: Toggle MRAM and STT-MRAM. Toggle MRAM uses magnetic tunnel junctions to store data through magnetic states, offering fast read and write speeds. In contrast, STT-MRAM (Spin-Transfer Torque MRAM) relies on the manipulation of electron spin to change magnetic states, enabling higher density storage and better scalability. Both technologies promise lower power consumption and faster performance compared to traditional memory solutions, catering to diverse applications in computing and electronics.</p></p>
