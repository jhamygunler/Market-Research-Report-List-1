<p><ul><li>Group I</li><li>Group II</li><li>Group III</li><li>Group IV</li><li>Group V</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Base oils are categorized into five groups based on their refining processes and properties. </p><p>Group I contains solvent-refined oils with less than 90% saturates and a high sulfur content. </p><p>Group II features hydrotreated oils with improved stability and lower sulfur content. </p><p>Group III consists of highly refined oils with higher viscosity indices, suitable for synthetic blends. </p><p>Group IV includes polyalphaolefins, synthetic base oils with excellent performance characteristics. </p><p>Group V encompasses other base oils, including esters and naphthenic oils, used in specialized applications.</p></p>
