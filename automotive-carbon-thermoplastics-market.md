<p><ul><li>Polyamide (PA)</li><li>Polypropylene (PP)</li><li>Polyphenylene Sulfide (PPS)</li><li>Polyetherimide (PEI)</li><li>Polyether Ether Ketone (PEEK)</li><li>Polycarbonate (PC)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive carbon thermoplastics market includes several key types, each with unique properties. Polyamide (PA) offers strong mechanical strength and heat resistance, making it suitable for various components. Polypropylene (PP) is lightweight and cost-effective, ideal for interior parts. Polyphenylene Sulfide (PPS) provides excellent chemical resistance and thermal stability. Polyetherimide (PEI) boasts superior toughness and dimensional stability. Polyether Ether Ketone (PEEK) exhibits exceptional thermal and chemical resistance, while Polycarbonate (PC) offers impact resistance and clarity, suitable for safety applications.</p></p>
