<p><p>The rock climbing shoes market is primarily divided into two types: leather and synthetic materials. Leather climbing shoes offer excellent breathability, comfort, and a natural fit that molds to the foot over time, making them ideal for long climbs. However, they may stretch and require a break-in period. On the other hand, synthetic climbing shoes are often more durable, moisture-resistant, and tend to maintain their shape, providing a snug fit right out of the box, making them suitable for various climbing styles.</p></p>
